# STE Ontology — Overview

## The Single Substrate

The entirety of the observable universe is composed of one substance: **STE** — a finite, self-attracting potential fluid. There is nothing else. Every phenomenon we measure is STE behaving under its own single imperative viewed from a particular frame of reference.

STE is not *modelled as* a fluid — it **is** the perfect fluid. It is what teaches fluids to behave. Every fluid dynamic we observe in matter (viscosity, convection, vortex shedding, pressure) is inherited from the substrate underneath. Water acts like a fluid because the STE it is made of acts like a fluid first.

## The Single Rule

> **STE moves toward itself.**

That is the complete dynamics. There are no additional forces, no separate field carriers, no bolted-on constants. Everything below follows from this one behaviour applied recursively at every scale.

## What We Actually See

We never observe attraction directly — just as with gravity, we see only the *effect*, never the force itself. Successful attraction is invisible: STE flowing freely toward STE produces no signal, no particle, no photon.

Everything we can detect — particles, heat, light, mass, structure — is a **secondary effect** of attraction being frustrated. STE wants to be in one place, but it cannot. The entirety of observable existence is what happens when that collective pull toward unity fails to fully resolve:

- **Failure to flow** → compression, structure, particles
- **Failure to collect** → chokes, heat radiation
- **Failure to hold** → light, photon emission

The universe we measure is made entirely of frustrated attraction.

## From Rule to Structure

### 1. Free-Flow Regime (low concentration)

Where STE is spread thinly, it flows freely toward nearby concentrations. This is the substrate at its least viscous — what we experience as open space. The attraction gradient is gentle, the flow unimpeded.

### 2. Concentration & Viscosity Gradient

STE can collect into higher local concentrations. As concentration increases, so does attraction, and thusly viscosity also increases. The relationship is monotonic: **more STE in a region → more viscous that region becomes**.

This is not an additional rule. It is a direct consequence of mutual self-attraction: every parcel of STE in a concentrated region is being pulled toward every other parcel from all directions simultaneously. The competing pull vectors resist net motion — the fluid *stiffens*.

### 3. Tension and Compression

Attraction between any two points is **tension** — a pull along the line connecting them. Consider an arbitrary boundary surface inside a concentration: STE on both sides is under tension toward the other side.

When many tension lines converge on a region and the geometry prevents bulk flow (attraction pulls equally from all directions), the converging tension cannot produce net motion. The result at the convergence point is **compression** — tension that cannot resolve into motion accumulates as pressure instead.

Compression doesn't just sit there. Every atom is a knot of stored, unresolved tension. When compressed structures meet, their tension fields interact — each tries to pull the other in, but the internal compression of each resists reorganisation. The result: the structures **redirect** each other. This is what gets the observable universe in motion. Every collision, every mechanical force, is compression meeting compression — stored tension rearranging through attraction alone.

### 4. The Full Spectrum

The system is scale-free in the sense that no absolute numbers matter. It is a continuum from:

| Low end | High end |
|---------|----------|
| Free-flowing STE spread across vast distances | Maximum-potential STE packed into a QGP-like shell around quarks |

Everything in between — atoms, molecules, planets, stars — sits somewhere on this single gradient.

## Frame-Dependent Observables

### Mass (inside view)

**Mass** is the value of STE concentration and the inertia it produces, **measured from inside a particle**. A particle's own STE resists changes to its state of motion because every internal parcel is attracted to every other internal parcel — diverting the whole structure requires overcoming all of those internal bonds simultaneously.

### Gravity (outside view)

**Gravity** is the same STE field attraction, **measured from outside a particle**. The concentrated STE inside a particle still attracts the free-flowing STE (and other concentrations) in its surroundings. We give this external view a different name — gravity — but it is the same substrate obeying the same rule.

### Why Gravity Is Hard to Pin Down

Gravity, like weight, is inherently an **interaction between two masses**, not a property of one. You cannot measure the gravitational field of an isolated mass without a second mass to register the attraction. This two-body dependence is why gravitational measurements are noisy and why *G* is the least precisely known fundamental constant.

## The Choke Mechanism

At high concentration, localised depressions form and are enveloped by higher-strength surrounding flows. The enveloped parcels shed from the coherent bulk flow, curling into tight, self-referencing micro-vortices — **chokes**. A choke is a local discontinuity: it breaks the chain of flow the way a shed vortex behind a wing detaches from the laminar stream.

Critically, a choke is a **low-energy depression** relative to the active STE surrounding it. It has lost translational coherence and is lesser-attracting than the incoming flow. Active STE is therefore pulled past and around it — the stronger, more attractable fluid wins proximity to the local concentration, and the choke is left behind, bypassed outward.

### Heat as Choked STE

What we observe as **heat** is the signature of these shed micro-vortices. They retain kinetic character (vibration, spin) but cannot participate in directed flow. The aggregate agitation of a population of chokes is what we measure as temperature.

### Why Heat Rises

Choked nodes are lesser-attracting, so active STE is preferentially pulled inward past and around them, leaving them progressively farther from the local pull centre. "Heat rises" is not a separate law; it is STE self-attraction performing triage — the most attractable fluid wins the innermost position, and the choked remainder is simply left behind.

## Discrete Structure & Self-Regulation

As soon as local conditions create an opportunity for discrete measurement — a boundary, a standing-wave condition, a sufficient concentration gradient — STE attempts to collect into a single point. When it **cannot** (because attraction pulls from all sides simultaneously, creating competing vectors), the failure crystallises into **structure**. Every particle, shell, and node is a monument to frustrated attraction — STE that wanted to be in one place but could not get there.

This is why we can see matter at all: successful attraction is invisible; only failure radiates, structures, and persists as something measurable.

Under normal circumstances, this constructive tendency is **held in check by itself**. The same attraction that drives concentration also creates the choke that limits further concentration. The system is self-regulating:

> **Attraction → Concentration → Choke → Equilibrium**

Every stable structure (proton, atom, planet) is a dynamic equilibrium on this cycle. Disrupt the balance and the system adjusts — sometimes catastrophically (supernovae), sometimes gently (thermal radiation) — always converging back toward the nearest available equilibrium.

## Choke Lifespan — How a Micro-Vortex Lives, Travels, and Dies

A choke is born when a localised depression gets enveloped by higher-strength surrounding flows. The mismatch forces the field to curl, spawning a tiny tornado-like micro-vacuum with rotational flow around it.

Its lifespan follows a predictable sequence:

1. **Formation** — A localised depression gets enveloped by higher-strength flows. The mismatch creates a micro-vacuum, causing a rotational flow around it.
2. **Lift-off** — The surrounding field flows *around* the obstruction instead of *into* it. The shear lifts the vortex away from the core, exactly the way a tornado funnel detaches from the ground.
3. **Coherence zone** — The vortex migrates outward until it reaches a radius where inflow, shear, and background density balance. This is the only place it can maintain its identity.
4. **Drift** — The vortex wanders within the coherence zone — pulled inward by compression, drifted outward when excited.
5. **Dissolution** — If it strays too far outward, the ambient STE density matches or exceeds the vortex's core. The rotational shell is more attracted to the denser surrounding regions than to its own low-energy centre — the shell gets stripped away, the vortex collapses, and its STE re-equalises with the substrate.

A choke's death is simply the loss of the tension gradient that defines it. No gradient, no vortex.

## The Electron Cloud — A Swarm of Micro-Vortices

An electron is not a single vortex. It is a **swarm** of micro-vortices, each one a choke with its own lifespan. The electron's identity is the collective behaviour of this swarm — individual vortices die and reform continuously, but the ensemble persists.

This gives the electron every property we observe:

- **Size** — The cloud is the coherence zone: the radial shell where micro-vortices can survive. Too close to the nucleus is too dense; too far out is too uniform.
- **Stability** — The swarm reinforces itself statistically. Turnover of individual vortices does not destroy the collective.
- **Compression response** — When squeezed, the swarm shifts inward, redistributes circulation, and sheds surplus tension as photons.
- **Excitation** — When energised, vortices drift to higher coherence radii, expanding the cloud.
- **Ionisation** — If enough vortices drift beyond survivable radius and dissolve, the cloud loses its bound identity.
- **Wave-like behaviour** — The swarm's spatial probability distribution over the coherence zone **is** the wavefunction. Quantum fuzziness is not mysterious; it is the statistical footprint of a living, breathing vortex population.

Electrons exist wherever the substrate's self-attraction naturally forms and sustains micro-vortex swarms — anywhere concentration gradients and background density permit them to persist.
